## Take home exam

### Practical information

In the exam you will write a statistical report. Think about it as a report that you write for a collaborator who knows basic survival analysis, but wants results and interpretations explained. Your collaborator doesn’t know R nor have access to the data set. Write in clear and concise academic English or Danish. The text should not use R language, commands or direct output. Avoid referring to variable names from the data set. That is, don’t use terms like “coxph” or “prodlim”.  Refer to variables by their medical/epidemiological terms and not the column names in the data set (if they differ). You are very welcome to include your R code in an appendix at the end of the report.

The exam is to be written in groups of 1-3 members. The individual contributions of each member must be clearly identifiable; the main responsible member of each section should be specified on a separate page (that isn’t included in the page count). Up to one third of the report may be written under joint responsibility. The report is expected to be 5-7 pages long (including tables and graphics, but not appendices We don't count words or characters.). Do not exceed the page limit.

The exam is graded as passed or failed.

For groups it is sufficient and preferred that you hand in only one pdf for the entire group. 

### The assignment

PhD students can choose to do the assignment provided by us (to appear at the very end of the course) or work on their own data. We encourage PhD students to work on your own data. Write a report where you analyze your own data using the methods and techniques from the course (including model checking). The emphasis should be on interpretation and communication of results. If you cannot use your own data, you can instead base you report on an exam assignment that will appear here at the end of the course.
